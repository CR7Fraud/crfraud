About Us

Welcome to the portal dedicated to sports truth. This site is born from a commitment to critical analysis, solid statistical data, and fair comparisons between athletes to expose the reality behind Cristiano Ronaldo's career.

The goal is to demystify titles, awards, and controversial decisions, transparently showing that many achievements attributed to Ronaldo go far beyond individual merit. Here we explore external situations, favoritism, questionable administrative decisions, regulation manipulation, and behind-the-scenes events that helped create an image of success often misaligned with the facts.

Nothing here is based on fanaticism or biased opinions: every argument is built on real numbers, verified episodes, and historical events, without the filters of media narrative or idolatry. Everything exposed is undeniable - 100% true, logical, and supported by evidence visible to all, though often ignored in common debates.

The mission of this project is to open people's eyes, awaken critical awareness, and encourage everyone to question the modern football system and athlete recognition. The content presented makes irrefutable the reality about Ronaldo: titles, trophies, and prestige obtained without real justification, and the deconstruction of a farce built over the years.

I will also ensure that everyone sees that Lionel Messi is light years ahead, and that Ronaldo shouldn't even be in the world's top 3 of history. The pure truth, free from fanaticism and interests, will be revealed here with facts, numbers, and analyses that leave no doubt.

Reject illusions and reflect on sports justice. This portal invites you to see the facts, far from blind beliefs or external interests - because truth alone is enough.

ZERO Trophies & Ballon d'Or Voting Dates Change

Ronaldo: Ballon d'Or Without Trophies
Ronaldo won his second Ballon d'Or in 2013, with the extraordinary record of ZERO trophies won that season.

Meanwhile, the other two nominees (Messi and Ribery) won several trophies and had excellent individual numbers.

Ronaldo was not the best player in any competition.
Impact in Champions League & National Team
In the Champions League semifinals, he lost 4-1 to Borussia Dortmund in the first leg, scoring one goal, but in the second leg, Ronaldo didn't score and Real Madrid were eliminated.

For the national team, in the 2014 World Cup qualifiers, he wasn't the top scorer in the group stage (27th place, 8 matches, 4 goals) nor in the playoffs (2 matches, 4 goals).

Ronaldo was only truly decisive in the playoffs, scoring a hat-trick against Sweden to qualify Portugal for the 2014 World Cup.
Voting Deadline and Controversy
For the first time, the Ballon d'Or voting deadline was postponed right up to the Portugal vs Sweden game.

How did Ronaldo win that Ballon d'Or? It could have gone to Ribery or Messi, since both had years full of achievements and good stats, but the award went to someone who won nothing.

Did he win it just because of his 69 goals? If that's the case, that's what the Golden Boot is for.

Unlucky Messi
2014 was another year full of controversy about Ronaldo winning the Ballon d'Or and it is indeed debatable.

He won best player at the World Cup, one of the most important individual trophies.
Champions League and Decisive Moments
Ronaldo had better raw numbers, was Champions League top scorer, broke goal records, and won major titles with Real Madrid.

But key players around him were decisive in major moments: Bale, Ramos, and Di Maria had massive impact in finals. The debate is whether goals alone should define Ballon d'Or merit.
Debate: Who Truly Deserved It?
Neuer had a world-class year and won the World Cup as best goalkeeper. Messi was best player at the World Cup and maintained elite numbers.

The core argument here is that Golden Boot is not the same as Ballon d'Or merit, and context matters.

Team Glory vs Individual Brilliance

Ronaldo: Trophies but Controversy
2016 was an "easy" year for Cristiano Ronaldo because he won the Champions League and Euro 2016, but the discussion here focuses on impact and context.

In the Champions League, much of the scoring came in the group stage, with limited contribution in semifinal and final moments.
Euro 2016 & Ronaldo's Impact
Portugal advanced from the group stage without wins, and in the final Ronaldo left injured after 25 minutes while Portugal still won 1-0.

The argument here is that he was not the central figure in every decisive moment.
Messi's Year & Ballon d'Or Debate
Messi had elite stats, major domestic titles, and strong tournament performance. The comparison emphasizes individual influence versus team trophy narrative.

2017

Both had good seasons. Messi had standout creativity, Golden Boot output and playmaker recognition. Ronaldo had a major title haul and strong Champions League run with decisive goals in late rounds.

The argument here recognizes a strong case for both, but highlights that title weight drove this result.
